Reflection

To me, "Writing in the Digital Media" sounded like the methodology and approach of writing online journalism. In retrospect, I couldn't have been further from the truth. Granted that's probably a slight overstatement, my mental compass was certainly pointing my expectations in the wrong direction when I arrived to the first day of class. Fortunately, this new direction revealed a more beautiful horizon: HTML.

Like the most new concepts, coding didn't sound too achievable or learnable. In fact, before this class, I thought coding was secluded for those ingenious hackers you see in the movies opening maximum security doors from their bedroom closets.

However, I pleasantly learned that coding HTML was extremely intuitive and understandable. Despite having only scratched the surface, the tools and abilities of HTML are numerous.

The modular readings have provided a new perspective and appreciation for what I'm doing in class. To know that I'm working with the very idea that Berners-Lee planted at CERN twenty-seven years ago is somewhat incredible –– how far technology has come is even more so.

Loren Skinker
