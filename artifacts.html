Artifacts

Podcast


My group created a podcast that narrated the story of an incoming engineering freshman, Billy, who is extremely ignorant and intolerant of other majors, especially communications and english. However, Billy is assigned to write a biography in one of his introductory engineering classes. Struggling to string together a coherent story, Billy begrudgingly visits the Writing Center where he is counseled by a girl who is majoring in both engineering and english. By the time the paper is due, Billy realizes that a diverse skill set of practical and liberal knowledge is vital to a student's success, no matter the major. In recognizing this, Billy understands the importance of the T-shaped student. The vertical part of the T represents a student's interdisciplinary skills while the horizontal hits on the broader knowledge of other secondary talents.

Video


Our video project tells the story of an english major who struggles to find an internship because of his lack of involvement in extracurricular activities. After approaching a Red Cross booth at Business Horizons with an unprepared resume, Jimmy realizes that although GPA and academics are don't make up a student entirely. The representative instructs that Jimmy to get involved in campus organizations and participate in community service events at Virginia Tech. She then notifies him that many employers like to see how a student is taking what they have learned in class and putting it to good use outside academia's walls. Over the next year, Jimmy volunteers in a variety of Virginia Tech's events and services including Big Event, Relay for Life, and Hokie Helpers.
